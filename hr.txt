
# HR Manual

## 1. Introduction

Welcome to Acme Corporation! This HR manual is designed to provide you with the essential information about our company's policies, procedures, and benefits. This manual will help you understand your role and responsibilities as an employee of Acme Corporation.

## 2. Company Overview

Acme Corporation is a leading provider of innovative solutions in the technology sector. Our mission is to deliver high-quality products and services that exceed customer expectations. We are committed to creating a positive and productive work environment where every employee can thrive.

## 3. Employment Policies

### Equal Employment Opportunity
Acme Corporation provides equal employment opportunities to all employees and applicants without regard to race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, or genetics. We are committed to diversity and inclusion in the workplace.

### Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination
Our company strictly prohibits any form of harassment or discrimination in the workplace. This includes, but is not limited to, sexual harassment, racial discrimination, and any other form of harassment based on protected characteristics.

### Recruitment and Hiring
We follow a structured and fair recruitment process to attract and retain talented individuals. All job openings are posted internally and externally, and we encourage employees to refer qualified candidates.

### Employment Categories
Employees are categorized as full-time, part-time, or temporary. Full-time employees work 40 hours per week, part-time employees work less than 40 hours per week, and temporary employees are hired for a specific period or project.

### Working Hours and Overtime
Our standard working hours are from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, Monday to Friday. Overtime is compensated according to labor laws and requires prior approval from your supervisor.

### Attendance and Punctuality
Regular attendance and punctuality are essential to the smooth operation of our business. Employees are expected to notify their supervisor as soon as possible if they are unable to attend work or will be late.

## 4. Compensation and Benefits

### Salary and Wages
Employees are paid on a bi-weekly basis. Salary reviews are conducted annually based on performance and market conditions.

### Health and Wellness Benefits
We offer comprehensive health and wellness benefits, including medical, dental, and vision insurance. Employees are also eligible for wellness programs and gym memberships.

### Retirement Plans
Employees are eligible to participate in our 401(k) plan with company matching contributions. Details about the plan and enrollment are provided during orientation.

### Paid Time Off (PTO)
Employees accrue paid time off based on their length of service and employment category. Full-time employees earn 15 days of PTO per year, which can be used for vacation, personal days, or sick leave.

## 5. Employee Conduct and Discipline

### Code of Conduct
Employees are expected to maintain high standards of professional behavior. This includes adhering to company policies, treating colleagues with respect, and performing job duties to the best of their abilities.

### Disciplinary Actions
Failure to adhere to company policies may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination. Disciplinary actions may include verbal warnings, written warnings, suspension, and termination.

## 6. Performance Management

### Performance Reviews
Regular performance reviews are conducted to provide feedback and set goals. Employees will meet with their supervisors annually to discuss performance, set objectives, and identify development opportunities.

### Training and Development
We are committed to the continuous development of our employees. Training programs, workshops, and seminars are available to help employees enhance their skills and advance their careers.

## 7. Health and Safety

The safety of our employees is a top priority. We follow all relevant health and safety regulations and provide regular training to ensure a safe working environment. Employees are expected to adhere to safety policies and report any hazards or incidents immediately.

## 8. Employee Relations

### Open Door Policy
Employees are encouraged to speak freely with management about any concerns. Our open door policy ensures that employees can discuss issues or suggestions directly with their supervisors or HR.

### Grievance Procedures
Formal procedures are in place for addressing employee grievances. Employees should submit a written complaint to HR, who will investigate the matter and take appropriate action.

## 9. Termination of Employment

### Resignation
Employees are expected to provide at least two weeks' notice when resigning. This allows for a smooth transition and proper handover of responsibilities.

### Involuntary Termination
Involuntary termination may occur due to performance issues, misconduct, or business needs. Employees will be provided with a written notice and the reasons for termination.

### Exit Interviews
We conduct exit interviews to gather feedback from departing employees. This feedback helps us improve our policies and work environment.
